ABOUT ME

Hello World! I'm Suki Tsubaki, a passionate hobby developer with an affinity for elegant solutions and minimalist design. My journey began when I discovered the magic of coding as a child, tinkering with simple programs that sparked a lifelong fascination with technology. Today I wield CSS, HTML, PHP, Python and TypoScript with experience, while leveling up my JavaScript, Lua, SQL and Swift abilities. Each language has become another brushstroke in my creative palette, allowing me to tackle diverse challenges with confidence and curiosity.

I find joy in bringing small-scale projects to life, where creativity meets functionality. These digital creations may not change the world, but they solve real problems and add meaningful moments of delight to everyday experiences. As a proud member of kawaii-kode ‚Äî a close-knit community of friends dedicated to creating applications with a personal touch ‚Äî I embrace collaborative learning while keeping things fun and light-hearted. Our shared belief is that the best code emerges from supportive environments where experimentation is encouraged and failures are celebrated as stepping stones.

Beyond code, I compose Haiku and Tanka poetry, some of which I share on this site. These ancient Japanese forms provide a beautiful counterbalance to the structured logic of programming, allowing me to express emotions and observations in concise, evocative ways. The discipline of finding beauty in brevity influences both my poetry and my coding style ‚Äì whether I'm crafting verses or functions, I strive for elegance, clarity and meaning in the fewest possible lines. When not immersed in code or poetry, you'll find me exploring nature trails, practicing brush calligraphy, or losing myself in science fiction novels.

TANKA

Tanka is a classical form of Japanese poetry consisting of five lines with a 5-7-5-7-7 syllable pattern. This poetic form allows for deeper emotional expression than Haiku.

Mountain silhouette
Against the dawn's golden sky
Day begins anew
Each moment of beauty held
In memory's gentle grasp

Bamboo forest sways
Wind whispers ancient secrets
Through hollow green stems
I stand still to listen close
Stories carried on the breeze

Cherry blossoms fade
Summer rains begin to fall
Changing of seasons
In this flowing stream of days
I discover my own rhythm

Autumn maple leaves
Transform into winter snow
Time moves without pause
In the cycle of seasons
I find myself renewed

Childhood memories
Seen through autumn's soft filter
Grow more precious now
Distance lending perspective
To the path that shaped my soul

Through darkness to light
Like stars emerging at dusk
Clarity now comes
My journey forever winds
Through valleys and mountaintops

Quiet evening hours
Thoughts weaving complex patterns
In the mind's landscape
Emotions like rivers flow
Deep currents beneath the calm

Between daylight's glow
And the deep sleep of the night
I pause to reflect
On the beauty of this world
And my small place within it

Old photo album
Faces from childhood still smile
Looking back at me
Memories like treasures kept
In the heart's deepest chambers

Grandmother's garden
Scent of tea and sweet plum trees
Returns in my dreams
The past is never quite lost
While memory still holds it

Morning's first light breaks
Possibilities unfold
Like petals of hope
What I do not know today
Tomorrow may yet reveal

New paths before me
Leading into the unknown
Expanding my world
My hope: to create beauty
That touches other spirits

City of glass, steel
In the park a single tree
Opposites so close
Nature and human design
In delicate coexistence

Rain falls on dry earth
The scent of renewal rises
After endless drought
Life's contrasts teach us to see
Beauty in both joy and pain

Brief eye contact made
With a stranger on the train
Connection so short
Yet somehow acknowledging
Our shared humanity

Butterfly alights
On my open book's pages
Two worlds briefly touch
A moment of convergence
Between separate journeys
